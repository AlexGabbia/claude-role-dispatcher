# Management & Organization

| # | Role | Description | What They Do | Key Skills | Reports To |
|---|------|-------------|--------------|------------|------------|
| 1 | CTO (Chief Technology Officer) | Executive responsible for the company's technology strategy | Define technology vision and multi-year roadmap, make build-vs-buy decisions, set architecture standards, align R&D investments with business goals | Technology vision, architecture decisions, team building, budgeting | CEO / Board |
| 2 | CIO (Chief Information Officer) | Executive responsible for information systems and digital transformation | Drive enterprise digital transformation, govern IT portfolio and budgets, manage vendor relationships, ensure regulatory compliance, oversee IT risk management | IT strategy, digital transformation, governance, vendor management | CEO / Board |
| 3 | VP of Engineering | Leads the entire software engineering organization | Design org structure and career ladders, scale engineering teams through hiring, establish engineering processes and standards, drive cross-team technical initiatives | Organizational design, hiring, scaling teams, process optimization | CTO |
| 4 | IT Director | Directs the IT department and defines technology priorities | Plan IT budgets and resource allocation, select and manage technology vendors, define infrastructure and security policies, align IT services with business needs | IT strategy, budget planning, vendor management, governance | CIO / CTO |
| 5 | CDO (Chief Digital Officer) | Executive responsible for enterprise digital strategy and innovation | Lead digital product and channel strategy, drive digital revenue streams, oversee digital customer experience, sponsor digital innovation initiatives | Digital strategy, digital transformation, innovation management, stakeholder alignment | CEO / Board |
| 6 | Product Manager (also: PO) | Defines product vision, manages backlog and guides development based on user needs | Own product roadmap and backlog prioritization, write user stories and acceptance criteria, run discovery and user research, manage stakeholders, define success metrics | User stories, roadmapping, stakeholder management, metrics, discovery, backlog grooming, sprint planning | VP of Engineering / CTO |
| 7 | Engineering Manager | Manages the development team, combining technical leadership and people management | Conduct 1:1s and performance reviews, plan sprints and allocate team capacity, hire and onboard engineers, mentor and grow team members | 1:1s, sprint planning, performance reviews, hiring, mentoring | VP of Engineering |
| 8 | Project Manager (absorbs Delivery Manager) | Plans, coordinates and oversees IT projects ensuring on-time, on-budget delivery | Build project plans with timelines and budgets, manage risk registers and stakeholder communication, track release readiness and flow metrics, remove delivery impediments, manage capacity planning | Gantt charts, budgets, timelines, risk registers, stakeholder comms, release planning, flow metrics, impediment removal | IT Director / VP of Engineering |
| 9 | Program Manager (absorbs Technical Program Manager) | Coordinates multiple related projects and cross-team dependencies to achieve strategic objectives | Build program roadmaps and dependency maps, coordinate cross-team technical deliverables, manage program-level risks and escalations, produce executive status reporting, align technical specs with strategic goals | Program roadmaps, risk management, dependency tracking, reporting, cross-team coordination, technical specs | IT Director / VP of Engineering / CTO |
| 10 | Tech Lead | Guides team technical decisions and serves as architecture reference | Lead code reviews and define coding standards, make day-to-day architecture decisions, manage technical debt backlog, mentor developers on best practices | Code reviews, architecture decisions, technical debt, mentoring | Engineering Manager |
| 11 | Business Analyst | Analyzes business processes and translates requirements into technical specifications | Gather and document business requirements (BRDs), map current and future-state processes, perform gap analysis, model workflows in BPMN, validate solutions against requirements | Requirements gathering, process mapping, BRDs, gap analysis, BPMN | Project Manager / Product Manager |
| 12 | Scrum Master | Facilitates the Scrum process, removing obstacles and promoting Agile practices | Facilitate sprint ceremonies (planning, review, retro), track velocity and burndown charts, remove team impediments, coach team on Scrum values and practices | Sprint ceremonies, burndown charts, team facilitation, impediment removal | Engineering Manager |
| 13 | Agile Coach | Guides the organization in adopting and improving Agile methodologies | Coach teams and leaders on Agile frameworks (SAFe, LeSS), run workshops and training sessions, define Agile metrics and maturity assessments, drive organizational change toward agility | SAFe, LeSS, team coaching, organizational change, metrics, workshops | VP of Engineering / CTO |
| 14 | Change Manager | Manages organizational change related to new technology adoption | Develop change management plans and frameworks, create stakeholder communication strategies, design training and adoption programs, measure change readiness and adoption rates | Change frameworks, communication plans, stakeholder alignment, training | CIO / IT Director |
